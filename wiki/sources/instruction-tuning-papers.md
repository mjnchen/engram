# Instruction-Tuning-Papers

## Summary

Tracks the wave of instruction-tuning papers that followed InstructGPT and FLAN, showing how fine-tuning on task instructions transfers to unseen tasks. The collection makes clear that the field shifted from training on labeled data to training on natural language instructions that describe the task.

## Key Points

- FLAN-style multi-task instruction tuning improves zero-shot generalization measurably
- Self-instruct methods (generating synthetic instructions from the model itself) reduce reliance on expensive human annotation
- Instruction diversity matters more than instruction count above a certain threshold
- Open alternatives (Alpaca, Vicuna) used distillation from GPT-4 outputs as a shortcut
