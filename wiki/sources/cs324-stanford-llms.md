# CS324 - Large Language Models (Stanford)

## Summary

Stanford graduate course from winter 2022 covering LLMs from theory to societal impact, taught during the year GPT-3 made LLMs mainstream. The lecture notes remain a rigorous primary resource for understanding capabilities, training, harms, and legal/ethical considerations in a single structured curriculum.

## Key Points

- Covers scaling laws, emergent abilities, and the empirical science of model behavior systematically, not just as buzzwords
- Dedicated lectures on harms: bias, toxicity, privacy, misinformation — grounded in measurement methods, not just statements
- Adaptation section covers in-context learning, fine-tuning, and prompting with formal treatment of why they work
- Benchmarking lecture explains why standard benchmarks can be misleading and how to design better evaluations
