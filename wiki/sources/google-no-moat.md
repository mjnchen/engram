# Google "We Have No Moat" Leak

## Summary

Leaked internal Google memo from May 2023 arguing that open-source AI (driven by the Llama leak) had eliminated the competitive moats of large labs because fine-tuning at small scale could approximate large-model performance cheaply. The memo's core prediction — that open-source would rapidly close the quality gap — proved partially correct for some tasks and incorrect for others.

## Key Points

- LoRA-style fine-tuning on consumer hardware made the cost of customization nearly zero, disrupting the assumption that API access was necessary
- The memo argued that neither Google nor OpenAI had proprietary advantages that couldn't be replicated once weights were available
- Multimodality, RLHF quality, and very long-context capability remained harder to replicate and became the new moats
- The leak accelerated open-source investment and public discourse about proprietary vs. open model strategies
