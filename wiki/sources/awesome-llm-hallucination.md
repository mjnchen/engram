# Awesome-LLM-hallucination

## Summary

Curated paper list organizing hallucination research into causes, detection methods, and mitigation strategies. The categorization reveals that hallucination is not one problem but several — intrinsic (training data conflicts), extrinsic (facts not in training), and temporal (outdated knowledge).

## Key Points

- Separates hallucination by type: factual, faithful, and temporal
- Covers both white-box methods (logit-based detection) and black-box methods (consistency sampling)
- Mitigation strategies span retrieval augmentation, chain-of-thought, and RLHF-based corrections
- Detection remains harder than mitigation — models are often confidently wrong
